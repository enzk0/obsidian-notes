
**Gender Interests**
- interests that are developed by men or women *"virtue of their social positioning through gender attributes."*
- prioritized as gender concerns

**Gender Needs**
- *means by which their concerns may be satisfied"*
- Can be further differentiated into:
	1. Practical
	2. Strategic

**Practical Gender Needs**
- women's immediate needs
	- survival needs
	- nutrition
	- living conditions
	- health care
	- employment
	- etc.

**Strategic Gender Needs**
- *"needs women identify because of their subordinate position to men in their society"*
- more on the attainment of gender equality and women's empowerment

Addressing strategic gender needs involves an analysis of gender subordination

**Gender Mainstreaming**
- a major global strategy for ensuring that the government pursues gender equality in all aspects of the development process.
- can also be called **gender perspective**
- However in the Philippines, LBTQIA+ is not included in this.

**Human Rights Approach**
- is important/essential particularly for women's development as it serves as a means to increasing awareness about women: their plight and particular needs.

**The Universal Declaration of Human Rights**
- *"a common standard of achievements for all people and all nations"*

**The Convention of Elimination of all Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW)**
- also known as the **International Bill of Rights**
- *the only human rights treaty which affirms the reproductive rights of women and targets culture and tradition as influential forces shaping gender roles and family relations"*

**Millennium Development Goals**
- a collection of 8 goals that focus on major issues of the underprivileged people around the globe.
- drafted by UN in 2000
- focuses on reducing poverty, hunger, disease, and gender inequality, access to water and sanitation by 2015
- Highlight on women's needs
	- Goal 2, to achieve universal primary education
	- Goal 3, to promote gender equality and empower women
	- Goal 5, to improve maternal health

Republic Act 7192, or the Women in Development and National-Building Act

National Commission on the Role of Filipino Women (NCRFW), now the Philippine Commission of Women (PCW)
- to assist in *"in ensuring the formulation and nationwide implementation of gender-responsive government policies, programs, and projects"*

Executive Order No. 348 created the Philippine Development Plan for Women (PDPW) for the period 1989-1992
- the 1st development plan to integrate women's interest. In Philippine context.

The Philippine Plan for Gender-Responsive Development (PPGD)
- drafted to supplement PDPW

Philippine publication for GAD:
1. Harmonized Gender and Development Guidelines, 2010 by the National economic and Development Authority (NEDA), PCW, and the Official Development Assistance Gender and Development Network
2. Women's Empowerment, Development, and Gender Equality (EDGE), 2013-2016 by the PCW
