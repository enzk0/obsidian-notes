
Understanding Sex, Gender, and Sexuality
1. Sex
	- biological
	- refers to the physical attributes
2. Gender
	- A social and cultural construct
	- an expression, feeling, identity
	- composite behavior of men and women

What is Sex?
- Often referred as an act of reproduction
- It is something done for pleasure
- It is what people drive to do certain things

What is Gender?
- A socially learned behavior, usually associated with one's sex
- A social construct, to determine one's role
- Sex does not correspond to gender, rather it comes from the person and the society
- Gender role socialization, the process of learning and internalizing culturally approved ways of thinking, feeling and behaving
- External Regulations, involves various institutions dictating what is proper and normal based on one's identity
- Internalized social control, because of external regulations, causes a person to police himself or herself according to society's standards and norms
