
### Women Ways of Knowing
**Women and Silence**
Silence as knowing indicates an absence of thought or reflection

#### Received Knowledge: Listening to the Voices of Others
Received knowledge is developed by absorbing knowledge.
They listen and understand to what is being said enough for them to see words as imperative to learning.
Women who learn in this manner are able to do the *right* thing by following the *rules*

#### Subjective Knowledge: The Inner Voice and the Quest for Self
Women who learn through **subjective knowledge** learn to trust what is called their **inner voice and infallible gut**.
Women realize that following rules will not make them happy, moving from passivity to action.
Women trust on their **intuition** to decipher truth from fiction.

#### Procedural Knowledge: Voice of Reason and Separate and Connected Knowing
Learns through processes.
Women of this category learned well from formal systems of knowledge enough to excel.
Learners of this category focus more on the method and less of the problem.
Procedural knowers believe that each person has their own world view and is entitled to the person's own opinion.
**Understanding** then comes as a vital aspect of knowing.

#### Constructed Knowledge: Integrating the Voices
**Constructed Knowledge**, built upon the ability to reflect on and accept own self.

## Gender Fair Language
**Language**, a potent tool for how humans understand and participate in the world

### Violations of gender-Fair Language
**Sexist Language**, a tool that reinforces unequal gender relations through sex-role stereotypes, microaggressions, and sexual harassment.

#### Invisibilization of Women
- Generic use of MASC pronouns or the use of a MASC general
- Assumption that certain functions or jobs are performed by men instead of both genders
- Use of male job titles or terms ending in man to refer to functions that may be given to both genders

#### Trivialization of Women
- Brining attention to the gender of a person, if that person is a woman
	- *"girl athlete"*
- The perception of women as immature
	- *"darling, baby"*
- The objectification or likening to objects of women
	- *"honey, chick"*
